Web 3.0 - The Third Generation of Internet

Web 3.0 will focus on using a machine-based understanding of data to provide a data-driven and Semantic Web. The ultimate goal of Web 3.0 is to create more intelligent, connected and open websites.

Posted by Cary Bondoc on May 29, 2022

Unlike Web 1.0 where, the content creators were few and the vast majority of users simply acts as consumers of content, Web 2.0 brought us the Web as Platform, where software applications are built upon the Web as opposed to upon the desktop.

Web 2.0 business model relies on user participation to create fresh content and profile data to be sold to third parties for marketing purposes. Indeed, the internet has become a massive app store, dominated by centralized apps from Google, Facebook and Amazon, where everyone is trying to build an audience, collect data and monetize that data through targeted advertising. In my opinion, the centralization and exploitation of data, and the use of it without users meaningful consent, is built into Web 2.0 business model.

So what is Web 3.0 in a nutshell?

Web 3.0 will bring us a fairer internet by enabling the individual to be a sovereign. True sovereignty implies owning and being able to control who profits from one time and information.

Web 3.0 decentralized blockchain protocol will enable individuals to connect to an internet where they can own and be properly compensated for their time and data, eclipsing an exploitative and unjust web, where giant, centralized repositories are the only ones that own and profit from it.

"Web 3 is an internet owned by users and builders orchestrated with tokens." - cdixon

Also, in the coming days, it is presumable that the role of artificial intelligence will become wider than ever in the world of websites.

The Future of Web Development - Artificial Intelligence Integration

It can not be denied that AI will be the future of web development as this phase has developed at a higher speed. Currently, many leading companies have launched AI-specific. In addition, Gird is one of the most sought-after AI implementations that permits content-specific website development in no time. Furthermore, the launch of ADI tools has allowed automated website development which can be customized further according to the web developers.

Two common examples of AI systems are Apple's Siri and Google's Alexa have features of recognizing human speech, analyzing it by tracking patterns, and offering a better user experience thanks to information interpreted. One another example of the fastest-growing AI trends is speech recognition. It plays an essential role and an indispensable part in revolutionizing the mobile and website development world.
