Automation - The Enemy of Repetitive Tasks

Does the fear of making mistakes, and a possibility of being left behind are some reasons why older people are rejecting automation and implementation of new technologies in a company?

Posted by Cary Bondoc on May 28, 2022

A repetitive task can be defined as an action or work performed by a person that are repeated many times and is therefore boring.

Automation can help savvy companies grow the bottom line in a number of ways. During the COVID-19 pandemic, it's also proven essential for keeping businesses on track through supply chain disruptions, remote work, and many other challenges.

Few people enjoy repetitive tasks like data entry or email follow-up. Automating those tasks can free up employee's time to focus on the kind of innovative, value-added work that can only be done by a human, and that many find more satisfying. It's not surprising, then, that Zapier found that 90% of surveyed knowledge workers agreed that automation has improved people's lives in the workplace, or that 65% report that automating manual tasks has decreased their stress level.

So, if automation is good, why do some people don't like it?

It seems that the problem lies not in automation itself, but in adapting to the new process that was brought by automation. Some people hate that they will need to study again a process that is working perfectly for them.

“When technology takes over every aspect of our lives, we won't care anymore about the data but poems, songs, paintings and other creative arts.” ― Pooja Agnihotri

By default, the human nature is to resist new and disruptive changes. Technology is used in every facet of everyday life because it can provide the speed, connectivity, and efficiency to make tasks easier. Nowadays, everyone wants things to be easier and faster and for an older adult, it's important not to underestimate how technology can help them in their golden years.

The Bottom Line

These are the main reasons some people struggle with technology. But aside from these significant barriers that they may face, there is another very common one: they often mention the lack of instructions and guidance. The world has evolved, and it is becoming more and more important for them to keep learning.
