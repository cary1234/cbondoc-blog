KodeGo Bootcamp - IT Career in just as early as 12 weeks?

Being in the IT industry is one of the highest paying jobs, but is it possible to have a career in IT in just 12 weeks?

Posted by Cary Bondoc on May 28, 2022

The part-time full-stack web developer program of KodeGo has a span of 16 weeks, which is equivalent to 4 months. Due to this, I'm expecting to finish the program around August 15, 2022.

We started the boot camp in KodeGo full-stack web development last April 25, 2022. If my memory serves me right, due to unforeseen circumstances we were not able to conduct classes for more than a week (April 27 to May 9).

Every Cloud Has a Silver Lining

I was able to utilized those time of having no class to focus on my former job as an electrical engineer at our company as my resignation took effect on May 20, 2022.

For the past weeks, KodeGo helps me to kickstart my skills as a front-end web developer while integrating useful applications, extensions, and version controls system.

"If you cannot see where you are going, ask someone who has been there before." — J Loren Norris

Way back 2014, as a lone web developer / software engineer in a company I can finish creating websites around three weeks to one month. I never expected that simple websites that is responsive can be done in just a day or two.

Identifying the Hindrances

The problem lies in lack of mentorship, I am dedicated and passionated on my job, but being in my self without guidance from someone more knowledgeable than me is a dead end. So far, KodeGo community fulfills that lacking part of me and I'am grateful that I've made the right decision to join this boot camp.

“Mentoring is a brain to pick, an ear to listen, and a push in the right direction.” — John Crosby
